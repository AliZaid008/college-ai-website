Section 1: AI & Machine Learning

AI is the simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems.

AI is the broad concept of smart machines, while Machine Learning (ML) is a subset where machines learn from data without being explicitly programmed.

Deep Learning is a type of ML based on artificial neural networks with multiple layers (hence "deep").

Natural Language Processing (NLP) helps computers understand, interpret, and manipulate human language (like ChatGPT!).

It is a field of AI that enables computers and systems to derive meaningful information from digital images and videos.

A series of algorithms that mimic the operations of a human brain to recognize relationships between vast amounts of data.

A type of ML where the model is trained on labeled data (it knows the answers beforehand).

A type of ML where the model works on data that is not labeled, finding hidden patterns on its own.

Alan Turing is often considered the father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence.
